What is Soham

Soham

Soham, in Sanskrit is a combination of two words sah- that, aham- I, means
“I am that immense universal divine energy”.

Modern times are exciting but life has become equally challenging. We are leading busy lives and becoming more distant from natural self. We are busy at work for most part of our lives for our livelihood and busy entertaining ourselves by over utilization of our senses and mind. As a result we hardly get time to look inwards and introspect. When faced with challenges we are struggling to find answers for ourselves and are increasingly looking for professional help for lack of time or lack of will to dive within. As a result we are unsure and uncomfortable about dealing with our brighter and darker sides alike.

STITHAPRAJNA

Equipoise- Balance of Emotions

A state in which, despite the extremes of the situations we face we are able to maintain the balance of emotions.

NISCHAYA BUDDHI

Ability to make clear decisions

Ability to make clear decisions once all relevant information is available, considered and understood.

SVADHARMA

One’s own duty

Once this complex skill is developed it helps us to understand our loyalties to our duty, to be patient and even enhance our productivity when we feel caught between bound by duty and following our dreams.

NISHKAMA KARMA

Performing actions without attachment to result.

We may set a goal and work towards it with sincere effort. . However, our actions should not be influenced by result. At the same time we cannot escape with complete inaction.

ATMAGAURAVAM

Respecting our real SELF.

The knowledge of our real inner SELF is only to be experienced by search within. Once that knowledge is realized we should maintain that knowledge constantly to be loving always.

TRIKARANA SUDDHI

Integrating the Triad of Idea, Speech and Action.

Once an idea is consistent with truth and is uttered, it should be acted upon.

Why Soham

Why

Dive inwards to discover your true potential.

Your deep dive to inner self begins here.

Healthcare across the world recognizes that mental wellbeing is an essential aspect of our healthy living. Research has shown the interdependence of mental health and physical health as they may reciprocally impact each other. Spiritual quest and awareness is essential to enhance our mental wellbeing. While physical and mental wellbeing are the means to lead a stable life, Spiritual awareness and wellbeing is essential to guide us in life with probing questions such as “Who am I?”, “What is the purpose of this life?” and, “Am I an independent being or an interdependent being?”


To achieve these goals in life we need to discover our true self and realize the immense potential within. Dr. Malladi Srinivasa Sastry, a senior consultant Psychiatrist, with his vast experience of working in different healthcare settings such as NHS in UK and IMH in Singapore, understood the need for Holistic mental and spiritual wellbeing to play a key role in realizing one’s potential. He brought together Science, Psychology, ancient and contemporary Spiritual knowledge along with his vast clinical experience to specially design a highly interactive and insightful “Soham- Inner Development training program”. In a serene ambience and expertly facilitated by Dr. Malladi, participants are empowered to explore their inner self and learn to apply the insights into practice in daily life. Our journey begins here to make our lives more enriching, healthy and purposeful.

Dr. Malladi Srinivasa Sastry

Psychologist by profession
Spiritualist by seeker

Completed his MBBS from prestigious Andhra Medical College, Visakhapatnam. He received higher training in Psychiatry in UK with dual recognition in General and Geriatric Psychiatry. Having worked in UK as consultant for 4 years in Northamptonshire NHS Trust he moved to Singapore in 2010 to work in the tertiary Psychiatric Hospital, Institute of Mental Health where he worked as a Consultant Psychiatrist and later became a Senior Consultant Psychiatrist.

During his time in UK and Singapore he has been active in teaching and research. He taught medical students, junior doctors and nurses and also gave several talks and lectures to doctors, medical staff and general public. In Singapore he has been faculty to Duke-NUS, YLL and LKC Medical schools. He also taught in GDMH in Singapore. As part of research he published articles in psychiatric journals and presented posters, gave talks and conducted workshops in International conferences. He was given best reviewer award by Singapore Medical Journal in 2014.

Dr. Malladi has been actively involved in positively contributing to society through his strong belief in practicing principles based on Spirituality and Self exploration. While in UK he was active in learning and teaching Samskritam (Sanskrit), an ancient Indian language, through a student led initiative as part of Samskrita Bharati. When he moved to Singapore he helped establish Samskrita Bharati, Singapore which is recognized for its efforts to teach adults and children through its highly interactive style of teaching. Dr. Malladi has conducted several spoken Samskritam workshops in various parts of Singapore and in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia.

While in UK, Dr. Malladi was instrumental in forming Diya Charity and was its Chair until he moved to Singapore. Diya Charity conducted fund raising activities for rehabilitative work in India.

With the idea of making quality healthcare accessible to rural India, Dr. Malladi started Arogyaraksha Clinic in a rural town, Yelamanchili, Visakhapatnam District, in 2016. Since then it has been serving several residents of this small town and its surrounding villages with enthusiastic contributions from specialist doctors visiting from Visakhapatnam. He also initiated the rural mental health program to conduct psychiatric clinics and raise awareness among public regarding mental health and primary healthcare. As part of this he regularly goes to schools, colleges and associations to give motivational and educational talks.

Having a deep interest in culture and spirituality he identified the need for humanity to understand the essence of this vast wisdom and develop the skills to practically apply this knowledge in their day to day life.
